The Attack Surface

The hiring form is an unmonitored input field. Every enterprise is running it without a signal layer.

Vector 1: AI-fabricated credentials

Mass-apply automation generates thousands of tailored applications per day. GPT-optimized CVs, synthetically matched role-fit language, overfitted skills sections. Every recruiter inbox is full of inputs that were machine-generated to beat the ATS, not to represent a real person.

Vector 2: State-sponsored insider threats

320+ confirmed DPRK IT worker cases (FBI/DOJ). Real stolen SSNs. Deepfake interviews. Okta, CrowdStrike, and Palo Alto's own portfolio companies were targeted through the hiring funnel. The attack clears every existing perimeter control.

Vector 3: Serial identity reuse

The same fabricated profile, iterated and resubmitted across hundreds of employers. No employer sees the pattern alone. Background checks verify the stated identity, not the pattern across submissions. The cross-company graph does not exist.

Why existing controls miss it

Identity verification (Persona, IDV). Confirms the person is real. Does not confirm the application is genuine. DPRK operatives pass Persona with real stolen SSNs.

Background checks. Verify what the candidate stated. Run after hire decision is made. Zero signal at the submission event.

ATS systems. Receive the application after submission. Never touch the surface where behavioral signals exist: the form fill itself.

Video interview tools (Clarity, HireVue). Operate at the video stage, after $1,900+ in process cost is already spent. Downstream of the attack.

41% of enterprises report having hired at least one fraudulent employee. The attack is already in the pipeline. The defense does not exist at the layer where it needs to be.

Product Architecture

Three layers. Each one architecturally unavailable to incumbents.

Layer 1: Behavioral Detection
Submission-time signals

JavaScript SDK captures behavioral signals during form completion: paste patterns, field edit sequences, tab behavior, timing distributions, device fingerprint anomalies. 17 detection rules built from 18 months of adversarial first-principles research. The corpus required building the attack to understand the defense.

Layer 2: Gap Assessor
Ongoing pipeline integrity

Text-based consistency engine re-runs at every downstream stage: phone screen notes, technical test scores, interview transcripts, manager assessments. Each new data point is checked against what the candidate originally claimed. Works on everything a recruiter writes down. No video required. No biometric exposure. Works on every stage Phenom's voice-dependent agent cannot reach.

Layer 3: Cross-Company Signal Graph
The network moat

Behavioral fingerprints from flagged applications are added to a cross-employer graph, with direct candidate consent. A fraud pattern that cleared one employer's ATS is checked against all others. The same fabricated identity that passed at Company A is flagged when it appears at Company B. No ATS, background check company, or employer-deployed tool can build this: cross-employer data sharing is contractually written out of every enterprise agreement they operate under.

Gap Assessor
Fourth card: consistency at scale

For every application, Sentriq builds a Candidate Signal Profile: a structured record of behavioral baseline, claimed qualifications, and downstream consistency across all stages. HR sees a trust signal summary. The full profile is available for Compliance audit. The same output, used differently by two buyers, closes the dual-buyer problem without requiring a second sales cycle.

Platform-agnostic. JavaScript SDK deploys in one line, upstream of any ATS. No ATS partnership required. No integration dependency. The same architecture Cloudflare uses to operate upstream of every web server: invisible to the target system, essential to what happens before it.
