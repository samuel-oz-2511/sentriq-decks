The signal destruction economy

AI resume tools and mass-apply bots form a fast-growing market. YC-funded Tsenta sends 1,500 applications per day for a single candidate. Every ATS ingests them without distinction. The problem is global: US, Europe, Israel, Japan, APAC.

The root problem: ATS has no quality layer

Your ATS shows what was submitted, not whether a human wrote it or the skills are real. Every ATS in the market has this gap: nobody has built the layer that sits upstream and surfaces it.

The category is Application Intelligence for Talent Acquisition. It does not exist yet.

Recruiting teams want confidence in the applications they already have, not more of them. The product is a quality layer, not a filter and not a fraud detector: it tells recruiters which submissions are worth their time.

What Sentriq becomes at scale

At 15 customers, the graph returns signal no single employer can see alone. At 100, coordinated campaigns are detected before reaching any one company.

Greenhouse controls ~30% of the market and their contracts legally prohibit cross-employer data sharing, a permanent structural constraint. Sentriq requires neutral ground and direct candidate consent: neither is available to any ATS.

The more companies join the network, the harder it becomes to defraud any of them. That is the platform.
