Executive Summary

The weight of evidence is unambiguous: the hiring pipeline is not an HR problem that happens to create security risk. It is an active attack surface, systematically exploited by nation-states, organized criminal networks, and opportunistic insiders. The evidence spans every authoritative category: FBI public service announcements, DOJ indictments with prison sentences, CISA advisories co-signed by allied governments, named Fortune 500 breaches, major threat intelligence reports from CrowdStrike, Mandiant, Microsoft, Palo Alto, Okta, and SentinelOne, formal regulatory guidance from the New York DFS, and a Gartner prediction that 1 in 4 job applicants will be fake by 2028.

The single most authoritative rebuttal anchor is the U.S. Department of Justice. The DOJ has indicted North Korean nationals and their American facilitators, prosecuted a US citizen to 102 months in federal prison, seized laptop farms across 16 states, and publicly named Fortune 500 companies including an American automaker, an aerospace manufacturer, and a Silicon Valley tech company as victims. This is not threat research. This is criminal prosecution. When a CISO says "that's HR's problem," the correct answer is: the DOJ disagrees, and they have the indictments to prove it.

Gartner predicts 25% of all candidate profiles will be fake by 2028. CrowdStrike responded to 304 FAMOUS CHOLLIMA insider threat incidents in 2024 alone. Amazon's Chief Security Officer -- not their Head of HR -- personally leads the company's defense against over 1,800 North Korean job applications intercepted since April 2024. Every other enterprise perimeter has a security owner and a product category. The hiring funnel does not. That is the gap Sentriq closes.
