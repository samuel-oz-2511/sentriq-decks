Section 01

The Problem

Every security tool enterprise companies buy protects the business after someone is hired. Nothing protects the hiring funnel itself.

Hiring at scale is a high-volume process where recruiters make decisions under time pressure. The volume of applications has grown 3-5x since 2023, driven by AI-assisted job applications. Inside that volume, there are six distinct threat categories, ranging from automated bots submitting 1,500+ applications per day to state-sponsored operatives who have passed video interviews and background checks before being discovered.
The problem is not that HR teams lack process. The problem is that the signal inside the inbound pipeline is degrading. More applications, lower average signal quality per application. Every stage of the hiring process costs money. A full US enterprise hire, from application through background check and onboarding, costs $3,200+ in direct process cost plus significant recruiter time. When a recruiter shortlists an application that should have been filtered at submission, that cost accumulates across multiple rounds before the problem surfaces.

Section 02

What We Built

A three-layer application integrity system that operates at submission and tracks through the full pipeline. Candidate Hiring Captcha.

The framing that matters

Sentriq evaluates applications, not candidates. The unit of analysis is a submission event, not a person. This matters legally (no biometric exposure, no GDPR Article 9 trigger) and technically (the same person submitting to different roles is treated as independent applications). We do not build a candidate profile. We do not do identity verification. We are the application integrity layer.

Layer 1: Passive Behavioral Baseline
Invisible to clean applicants. Visible to automated submissions.
Sentriq runs as the candidate fills the employer's application form. It captures typing dynamics, mouse movement, fill patterns, CV-to-job-description overfitting, mass-apply fingerprints, AI content markers, and timing anomalies. Clean applicants experience zero friction. The system is invisible to them. For automated submissions, bots, and AI-assisted agents filling forms, the behavioral signature is immediately distinct. Anomaly detected: Layer 2 triggered. No anomaly: passes through to ATS without any intervention.
Layer 2: Async Challenge
Calibrated to what this specific candidate claimed. Cannot be proxied.
A short task, framed as part of the application process. Scoring combines behavioral signals during challenge completion and content quality of the response. The challenge is generated specifically for the gap detected: the question requires private experiential knowledge that a proxy cannot fabricate. An AI assistant can write a plausible-sounding answer. What it cannot do is write what a specific person with a specific background would write about a specific decision they actually made. That is the design principle. Content scoring is outside the threat model of every known anti-detection tool.
Layer 3: Human Review
Full audit trail. Recruiter makes the final decision. Always.
A Sentriq analyst reviews the full signal stack for flagged applications. The recruiter receives the flagged case with analyst context, not raw alerts. Sentriq never rejects a candidate unilaterally. The employer makes every hiring decision. The audit trail is a compliance deliverable, not just internal logging: EU AI Act Annex III requires human oversight for all AI systems used in hiring. This is a procurement-enabling feature for every enterprise CISO and legal team that needs to sign off before deployment.
Gap Assessor: Ongoing Pipeline Coverage
The same engine re-runs at every stage downstream.
After the initial application is processed, the Gap Assessor continues through the pipeline. Every new text data point that enters the process is checked against what the candidate originally claimed: phone screen notes, test scores, interview transcripts, manager assessments, async written screens, email responses. The recruiter sees gaps that were missed at screen, surfaced before shortlist. A flag if a gap was not addressed in the interview. An auditable evidence trail from submission through placement. Text-only by design: no video required, no biometric exposure, GDPR-compliant, works on every stage the recruiter touches.

What We Detect

Six categories of application risk, from automated submissions to state-sponsored fraud. The signal spectrum, foundation to highest severity.

01 / Behavioral Baseline
Is a human filling this form?
Typing dynamics, mouse movement, fill patterns. Establishes human presence before any scoring begins. The foundation layer.
02 / Mass-Apply Bots
Automated volume attacks
1,500+ applications per day from the same fingerprint. Detectable before a human ever sees them in the ATS. Does not require document analysis.
03 / CV-to-JD Overfitting
Gaming the ATS scoring
Resumes hyper-engineered to beat applicant tracking system scores. A $400M market of services that sell exactly this capability. We reverse-engineer the output.
04 / Skills Anomaly
Claimed skills not supported by evidence
Title and listed skills that have no supporting behavioral confidence, timeline, or demonstrated knowledge. Inflation that looks clean on paper.
05 / Profile Distortion
AI-generated and fabricated credentials
Timeline manipulation, AI-generated CVs, false credentials. Real documents, fabricated history. The fastest-growing category since 2023.
06 / Proxy Fraud
State-backed infiltration
Stolen identities, live AI coaching during interviews. DPRK operatives passed four video interviews and a full background check at KnowBe4. The highest-severity category, relevant to every financial services and tech company.

EU AI Act
Annex III: Mandatory Human Oversight for Hiring AI
The EU AI Act classifies AI systems used in employment decisions as high-risk under Annex III. Deadline: August 2, 2026. Every enterprise deploying an AI screening or evaluation tool in the EU must have documented human oversight, an auditable decision trail, and the ability to explain any adverse output to the candidate. Sentriq's Layer 3 human review is the compliance response. The audit trail from submission through placement is a built-in product component. This is not an add-on. It is why enterprise procurement teams can sign off.
GDPR
Article 9: No Biometric Data. Article 22: No Automated Decisions.
Sentriq evaluates applications, not candidates, and collects no biometric data. We do not do facial recognition, voice analysis, or identity verification. Behavioral metadata during form completion (typing patterns, mouse movement) is process data, not biometric data under GDPR Article 9. Article 22 is addressed by Layer 3: no automated decision is final. A human analyst reviews flagged cases. The recruiter makes every hire. The system is designed from the ground up for GDPR compliance in regulated markets.
EEOC
Disparate Impact Monitoring
The EEOC's 2023 technical assistance document on AI in hiring establishes that employers are responsible for disparate impact if an AI tool causes differential outcomes by protected class, even if unintentionally. Sentriq's signals are behavioral and content-based, not demographic. The audit trail means any outcome distribution can be analyzed for disparate impact and defended. The challenge calibration is role-specific, not demographic. This is the due-diligence layer enterprise HR teams need before deploying any automated screening tool.
IL Law
Israeli Privacy Protection Law: Update 2025
The 2025 update to Israel's Privacy Protection Law brings it closer to GDPR standards: data minimization, purpose limitation, cross-border transfer rules. For Israeli companies deploying Sentriq on international candidates, the data handling framework is the same as GDPR. Sentriq holds only behavioral fingerprints post-analysis, not raw candidate data. The legal posture for Booking Holdings' Israeli operations is the same as for the EU operations.
One specific design decision that reflects all four frameworks: cross-customer pattern learning uses abstract behavioral fingerprints only. No raw candidate data ever crosses company boundaries. Customer A does not see Customer B's applicant data. The graph learns what fraud looks like without inter-customer data leakage. This is how the network effect is built inside a GDPR-compliant architecture.
Section 06

Technical Foundation

Seven distinct IP layers. Each builds on the previous. This is what Ben built.

The structural advantage is not the code itself. It is that the cross-company behavioral graph can only be built through commercial deployment. ATS vendors are contractually banned from cross-employer data sharing. No intelligence agency or well-funded lab can shortcut this: you need hundreds of companies voluntarily deploying the product and generating ground truth at the point of application entry. That corpus does not exist anywhere. Sentriq builds it by being the deployed product. The deployment is the moat.
1
IP Layer 1
Pre-Identity Passive Observation on a Fully-Owned Surface
Behavioral capture begins before any identity exists, while the visitor anonymously browses the Sentriq-hosted job posting. Typing rhythm, hesitation, scroll patterns, and browser fingerprint are captured before the subject knows they are being measured as a candidate. This produces an unguarded behavioral baseline. ATSs structurally cannot do this: their data model is keyed on a person who applied. No "anonymous session that later became a candidate" object exists in their schema.
2
IP Layer 2
Identity Binding: Anonymous to Named Subject
Because Sentriq owns both the posting page and the application form, the entire pre-submission behavioral record attaches to the named candidate at the moment of submission. This produces a two-state record: behavior before identity, behavior after. Structurally equivalent to the baseline-vs-test protocol used in screening interviews, executed automatically on every applicant, at no marginal cost, with no enrollment step required. Only possible because Sentriq owns both surfaces end-to-end.
3
IP Layer 3
Multi-Stage Longitudinal Record
Same subject observed across four distinct contexts over time: anonymous browsing, application submission, async challenge, interview transcripts. Inconsistencies between contexts are themselves a signal. A candidate who types one way in the application and differently in the challenge is a flag. Other vendors observe one moment. Sentriq observes the trajectory across multiple moments and treats deviations as evidence.
4
IP Layer 4
Self-Consistency Analysis of Biographical Claims
Application decomposed into atomic components (claimed roles, dates, technologies, scopes, achievements) and cross-referenced internally to find contradictions. Deterministic checks handle timeline arithmetic and skill coverage. LLM-assisted analysis handles narrative coherence. Works on a single dossier in isolation with no external database required. Catches the dominant failure mode of the AI era: a story that is plausible on the surface but does not hold together under structured probing.
5
IP Layer 5: Core Defensible IP
Targeted Personalized Challenge Generation
When a gap is detected, Sentriq generates a bespoke challenge for that specific candidate, that specific role, that specific gap. The question requires private experiential knowledge: decisions made, reasons, tradeoffs, things that did not work. Knowledge that exists only with the genuine subject. The "hard for an LLM" property is designed in, not emergent. The value sits in the deterministic decomposition layer, not in the LLM. Without the deterministic layer, LLM-generated questions revert to generic and are defeated easily.
6
IP Layer 6
Privacy-Preserving Cross-Customer Pattern Learning
Applications reduced to abstract structural and behavioral patterns (fingerprints, not contents). These patterns are compared across the full corpus to detect repeating fraud signatures, mass-submission bots, and known fake-candidate templates. Raw candidate data never crosses customer boundaries. Customer A never sees Customer B's data. The model learns what fraud looks like across the population without inter-customer data leakage.
7
IP Layer 7
Behavioral Verification of the Challenge Response
During challenge completion, Sentriq captures the second behavioral fingerprint of the now-identified subject. Three simultaneous checks: same-person consistency (does the challenge-taker behave like the same human who filled the application?), LLM-assistance signature (does the response pattern match someone reading or pasting from a generative tool?), and coaching signature (does the timing pattern match someone receiving off-screen help?). Content analysis alone can be fooled. Behavioral analysis alone has high false-positive rates. The combination is what makes the system robust.
