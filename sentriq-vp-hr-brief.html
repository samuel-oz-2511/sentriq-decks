The pre-application layer — what it means
Without it

A team posts a role, receives 200 applications, and begins reviewing. A phone screen costs $150. A technical assessment costs $300. A panel costs $1,800. Somewhere in that sequence, a mismatch surfaces. The team paid for every step between submission and discovery.

With it

By the time the recruiter opens the first file, a signal is already attached. The application that claimed four years of experience but was completed in nine minutes with 80% paste activity is flagged. A targeted challenge fires automatically. The recruiter knows before the phone screen.

No automated rejections. The system surfaces evidence. The recruiter decides. Human oversight at every gate.

The structural gap

Every tool in the TA stack evaluates what a candidate submitted. None of them observe how it was submitted.

The behavioral context present during the application itself, the signals that would tell a recruiter whether the work in front of them reflects the person behind it, disappears the moment the file is uploaded. What remains is a polished output that looks like every other polished output, and must be evaluated on its own terms by a recruiter who has dozens more in the same queue.

AI application tooling has made it trivially easy to produce a well-formatted, keyword-optimized application with no direct connection to the experience being claimed. The ATS scores it highly. The recruiter opens it. Six rounds later, a mismatch surfaces that was visible from the first interaction, but only in hindsight.

The gap is not a people problem. It is a tooling gap: no layer in the current stack is designed to surface signal at the point of submission, before any human time is spent.

What an application integrity layer does

A new category of tooling is forming around this gap. The defining characteristic is that it operates at submission, not at evaluation. It does not replace the recruiter's judgment. It gives the recruiter something to exercise judgment on.

Behavioral signal at form fill. How an application is completed, not just what it contains. Keystroke patterns, edit frequency, session behavior, copy-paste ratio. Observed passively, without any change to the candidate experience.
Claim consistency tracking across stages. What was stated at application is held against what surfaces in a screen, a test result, or an interview transcript. Gaps auto-flag without requiring a recruiter to remember or cross-reference manually.
Targeted clarification challenges. When a gap appears, a specific, narrow challenge fires automatically before the next expensive stage runs. Not a generic assessment. A question built for the exact inconsistency. 48 hours to respond.
Human decision at every gate. No automated rejections. The system surfaces evidence. The recruiter or hiring manager decides. Full audit trail for any compliance review. EU AI Act compliant by design.
Cross-company pattern detection. Over time, behavioral patterns seen across multiple employers produce a signal baseline no single employer can build alone. A candidate who has applied to 50 companies in 6 weeks looks different from one who applied to 3.
