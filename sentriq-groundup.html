Why competitors cannot replicate this

Greenhouse's enterprise contracts permanently isolate customer data. That architectural decision cannot be undone.

The moat is two mechanisms

Two things cross employer boundaries: behavioral model weights (updated by volume) and a pattern library built from CVs, job descriptions, and transcripts that surfaces inflated qualifications at the structural level. No individual record leaves an employer's environment.

The more companies join, the harder it becomes to manipulate any of them.

Behavioral session data builds with every application. It cannot be replicated by a competitor entering later.

Privacy by design. Signals are anonymized and aggregated. No individual candidate data crosses employer boundaries. Consent architecture follows GDPR Article 6(1)(f) legitimate interest framework.
