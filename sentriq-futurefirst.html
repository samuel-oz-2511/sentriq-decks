The Regulatory Landscape

High-risk AI systems in employment require transparency, human oversight, and full audit trails. Enterprise HR tools using AI for application review must comply or face up to 3% of global revenue in fines.

UK ATRS + EEOC (US)

Sentriq: Compliant by Design
Human always decides

Sentriq surfaces a signal, never a verdict. The recruiter makes the call. This satisfies EU AI Act Article 14 (human oversight) and EEOC automated decision guidance.

Application, not candidate

The unit of analysis is the submission event, not the person. No protected characteristics are assessed. This removes Sentriq from the highest-risk EU AI Act classification.

Audit trail built-in

Every signal, every flag, every recruiter decision is logged. Compliance officers get the documentation they need without changing the hiring workflow.

Gartner names identity verification as a top 2026 CHRO priority. EU AI Act enforcement makes compliant tooling a legal requirement, not a preference. The procurement conversation has already started inside enterprise legal teams.

A6  Network Effects / Data Moat

Data accumulated through doing the work.

Every application processed enriches the signal graph. This data cannot be bought retroactively. It can only be earned by being deployed first.

Phase 1: Single Employer
Behavioral baseline per role

Signal is calibrated within one employer's funnel. Useful. But no network effect yet. The moat has not started compounding.

Phase 2: First 10 Employers
Cross-org patterns emerge

Mass-apply behavioral fingerprints appear across companies. The same pattern submitted to HSBC, Nvidia, and Booking Holdings is now visible. No single employer could see it alone.

Phase 3: 50+ Employers
Self-reinforcing graph

A behavioral signature that fails at one employer propagates across the network. Each new employer makes the graph stronger. Each stronger graph makes the next employer easier to close.

Why it cannot be replicated

The graph belongs to Sentriq, not to any employer. ATS platforms receive applications after submission, from a single employer's funnel. They have never captured the submission event. Merging behavioral data across employers would require data they were architecturally never positioned to collect.

FutureFirst portfolio as Phase 1 nodes

FutureFirst portfolio companies become the first signal nodes in the cross-employer graph. Financial services and enterprise tech employers generate the highest application volume. Three to five logos in the first 19 months creates the behavioral baseline the graph needs to become self-reinforcing.
