How the commercial relationship works

This is a channel model, not a vendor procurement.

AMS does not procure Sentriq as a tool and bill it back to clients. AMS embeds Sentriq as a capability within its RPO delivery, charges clients a premium for the enhanced service, and shares in the value created. This is the model that scales.

Option A
White-label module
Sentriq runs under AMS branding as an AMS One capability. AMS owns the client relationship. Sentriq receives a per-application or per-engagement fee.
Option B (recommended)
Co-branded layer
Sentriq is surfaced as a named capability in AMS's RPO delivery: "Application integrity, powered by Sentriq." AMS charges a premium per engagement. Revenue share to Sentriq.
Option C
Client-direct with AMS intro
