Our Approach
A trust signal at the moment of submission. Before a human reads a word.
We call it Candidate Hiring Captcha. Like the CAPTCHA on every website -- it doesn't block anyone, it doesn't replace the recruiter's judgment, and it's invisible to genuine applicants. It simply distinguishes authentic human intent from fabricated submissions and surfaces that signal alongside the application.
Not a rejection engine. Every flag routes to a human. No automated decisions.
Not ATS-dependent. Works regardless of which applicant tracking system you use.
Not about the candidate as a person. The unit of analysis is the application event -- how it was submitted, not who submitted it.
No new workflow for recruiters. The trust signal appears alongside the existing application record.

Our Approach 4 / 7
How It Works
Four steps. No ATS integration. No new recruiter workflow.
01
Embedded at Submission
A lightweight SDK sits inside the application form -- career page or job board. No ATS integration required. The candidate sees nothing different.
02
Behavioral Signals Captured
At the moment of form submission: keystroke patterns, timing, copy-paste behavior, consistency across fields. Signals that only exist during form completion -- not in the resume itself.
03
Cross-Company Graph
Signals are cross-referenced against a shared behavioral graph across all employers. A pattern flagged at one company surfaces at the next. No single ATS can build this -- it requires neutral ground.
04
Trust Signal Delivered
A trust signal is appended to the candidate record inside the ATS. The recruiter sees the application they always saw -- with one additional signal alongside it. Their decision, fully informed.
The founding team built the attack infrastructure before building the detection layer -- running 30+ live experiments across real ATS systems. The detection signatures came directly from seeing what actually works on the offense.
