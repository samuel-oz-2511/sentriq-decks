The Gap

Every tool fires after the application arrives. Nobody is at the door.

The entire HR technology stack assumes the application is genuine. ATS, screening tools, video interviews, background checks -- they all process and act on the application. None of them question it at the moment it arrives.

By the time a recruiter reads a resume, a fabricated application has already passed the first gate. Every dollar spent downstream is spent on a pipeline that may not be clean.
The submission event is the only moment where behavioral signals exist -- how the application was completed, not just what it contains. After that moment, those signals are gone permanently.
No ATS captures this. No background check reaches back to it. It is the only layer in the hiring stack with no incumbent.
Our Approach

A trust signal at the moment of submission. Before a human reads a word.

We call it Candidate Hiring Captcha. Like the CAPTCHA on every website -- it doesn't block anyone, it doesn't replace the recruiter's judgment, and genuine applicants never notice it. It distinguishes authentic human intent from fabricated submissions and surfaces that signal alongside the application.
Not a rejection engine. Every flag routes to a human. No automated decisions on candidates.
Platform-agnostic. Works regardless of which applicant tracking system is in place.
The unit is the application, not the candidate. We analyze the submission event -- how it was completed, not who completed it.
No new workflow for recruiters. The trust signal appears alongside the existing application record in the ATS.

How It Works

Four steps. No ATS integration required. No new recruiter workflow.

01
Embedded at Submission
A lightweight SDK sits inside the application form -- career page or job board. The candidate sees nothing different. No ATS integration required.
02
Behavioral Signals Captured
At the moment of form submission: keystroke patterns, timing, copy-paste behavior, consistency across fields. Signals that only exist during form completion -- invisible in the final resume.
03
Cross-Company Graph
Signals cross-referenced against a shared behavioral graph across all employers. A pattern flagged at one company surfaces at the next. No single ATS can build this -- it requires neutral ground.
04
Trust Signal Delivered
A trust signal is appended to the candidate record inside the ATS. The recruiter sees the same application they always saw -- with one additional signal alongside it.
The research came from running the offense before writing the defense. The team mapped what fabricated submissions actually look like in practice, across 30+ ATS environments, before building a single detection rule. The corpus is proprietary. No competitor can replicate it.
