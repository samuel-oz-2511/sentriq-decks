Section 01

The Problem

The hiring funnel is the only undefended entry point into a global enterprise. Every other door has a guard. This one does not.

Every enterprise in the world spends enormous resources defending its perimeter. Email security. Identity verification. Access control. SIEM. Endpoint protection. And then the same enterprise posts a job on LinkedIn, and anyone on earth can submit an application. No verification. No behavioral baseline. No signal beyond what the applicant wrote themselves.
Since 2023, three forces have converged to make this the most exploited gap in enterprise security:
Three converging forces

1. AI-generated applications at scale. A single person can now submit 500 tailored applications in a day. The inbound pipeline at every large company has grown 3-5x. Recruiters are drowning in volume. Signal quality per application has collapsed.

2. Remote hiring as the default. Remote work made it normal to hire someone you have never met in person. State-sponsored operatives have exploited this. DPRK IT workers passed 4 video interviews and a full background check at KnowBe4 before being discovered generating revenue for the North Korean weapons program. This is not a theoretical risk.

3. Easy AI fabrication. A convincing, consistent, well-formatted CV with plausible work history, accurate company names, and tailored skills takes approximately 4 minutes to generate. No friction. The old detection approaches (look for keyword stuffing, check LinkedIn consistency) are obsolete. The new attacks overfitting the job description so precisely that ATS scoring systems rank fabricated applications in the top decile.

Every tool enterprise HR teams use fires after the application is already inside the pipeline. ATS systems, assessment tools, interview platforms, background checks: all of them assume the person who submitted the application is who they say they are. Sentriq sits at the moment of submission, before any cost is spent, before any recruiter invests any time.
You already named this in our call

You mentioned LinkedIn and Drushim as the earliest public signals of this problem. You are right: the Israeli job board data shows exactly this pattern. The volume inflection happened in Q3 2023. The signal quality degradation followed within 60 days. You were already reading those signals. Sentriq is the infrastructure that sits behind those signals.

Section 02

What We Built

A three-layer application integrity system. It runs at submission, tracks through the pipeline, and delivers one signal to the recruiter inside the ATS they already use.

The one framing rule

Sentriq evaluates applications, not candidates. The unit of analysis is a submission event. We do not do identity verification. We do not build a candidate profile. We are the application integrity layer: Cloudflare for the front door of hiring.

Layer 1: Passive Behavioral Baseline
Invisible to real applicants. Immediately visible to automated submissions.
Sentriq runs as the candidate fills the application form. Typing dynamics, fill patterns, CV-to-JD overfitting, mass-apply fingerprints, AI content markers, timing anomalies. Clean applicants experience zero friction. Bots, AI agents, and fabricated submissions produce a behavioral signature that is immediately distinct. No anomaly: passes through to the ATS. Anomaly detected: Layer 2 triggered.
Layer 2: Async Challenge
Calibrated to this specific candidate, this specific gap. Cannot be proxied by an LLM.
A short task, framed as part of the application process. Generated for the specific gap detected: requires private experiential knowledge. Decisions you actually made. Tradeoffs you lived. Things that did not work. An AI assistant can write a plausible-sounding answer. It cannot write what a specific person with a specific background would say about a specific moment they actually experienced. That is the design principle.
Gap Assessor: Ongoing Pipeline Coverage
The same engine, running through every downstream stage.
After submission, the Gap Assessor tracks the candidate through the full pipeline. Every new data point (phone screen notes, test results, interview transcripts) is checked against what they originally claimed. The recruiter sees a trust signal appended inside the ATS they already use. No new tool to learn. No new system to log into. One signal, in the right place, at the right time.

Every company that deploys Sentriq makes the detection smarter for every other company. The first deployment is the hardest. The hundredth deployment is the most powerful. And the structural moat is not the code: it is the corpus. The deployment IS the graph.

Step 1: SAP acquires 8fold
SAP already owns the talent intelligence layer
8fold does skills-based matching: it maps internal talent against open roles, and when no internal match exists, evaluates inbound candidates against the job description. SAP acquired it via SuccessFactors integration. 8fold operates at the APPLICATION layer and downstream. It does not operate at FORM FILL. It receives submissions; it does not analyze the behavioral moment of submission itself.
Step 2: SAP acquires SmartRecruiter
SAP already owns an ATS
SmartRecruiter is now part of the SAP SuccessFactors HCM suite. SAP has the ATS layer: where candidates are managed after submission. And the talent intelligence layer (8fold): where candidates are scored after submission. The one layer SAP does not have is PRE-submission. That is the application integrity layer. That is Sentriq.
The Gap: Pre-Application
The only layer SAP does not own
Sentriq captures the behavioral signal at the moment of form fill, before the application enters the ATS, before 8fold evaluates it, before SmartRecruiter manages it. If Sentriq is deployed before 8fold, every application that 8fold evaluates has already been integrity-checked. The SAP stack goes from "post-submission" to "pre-submission" in one acquisition. That is the deal. That is what we are building toward. 8fold's direct competitors also include some of our competitors, which means Sentriq and 8fold are not competing: Sentriq is what 8fold needs upstream to complete the value proposition for SAP enterprise customers.
The trigger is not a cold outreach to SAP. The trigger is HSBC or Booking Holdings signing a design partner agreement. When a Tier 1 bank is live on Sentriq, the story writes itself.
